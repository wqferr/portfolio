Who am I?

I'm a 20 years old male from Campinas SP, Brazil, and I'm currently studying Computer Sciences at the University of São Paulo campus in São Carlos. I'm expected to complete the degree (including the required internship) by December 2019.

Since 2016, I've been part of the Fellowship of the Game (or "FoG" for short), a game development group run by students. The group aims to exchange and disseminate knowledge and experiences among its members and community by organizing game jams, giving lectures, and managing other game related events to the general public.

HexCasters

HexCasters is, without a doubt, the project I hold the dearest to me. Its itch.io page can be found here. The development team consisted of

  • Me as project leader, programmer and designer;
  • Rafael "Lag" Clerici as graphical artist and designer;
  • Marjorie Saito as programmer; and
  • Bruno "Flango" Larsen as SFX designer and composer.

Developed with the help of the Fellowship of the Game and under my responsibility, it is a turn-based strategy game made in Unity.

In the 21st night of September (no, really) of 2018, it reached itch.io's top 10 most popular games under "turn-based strategy HTML5 games", competing with the entire platform's catalogue in that category.

The development process was smooth (as smooth as agile development can be, at least), and the end product is aesthetically pleasing, balanced, and fun to play.

We wrote devlogs throughout most of the development revealing new features and possible new additions, and builds were released on a weekly basis.
